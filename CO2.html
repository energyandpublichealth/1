Carbon Dioxide (CO2)

Carbon dioxide is an odorless, colorless, greenhouse gas

Emission Sources

Carbon dioxide has both natural and anthropogenic sources. Emissions of carbon dioxide from combustion of fossil fuels (coal, methane, gasoline, diesel) for electricity and transportation are by far the largest source of this GHG.

Health Impact

Being a GHG, rising levels of CO2 contributes to a warmer Earth. A warmer Earth drives climate change. The impact of climate change varies for different geographic locations. Impacts include much hotter summers causing increasing mortality rates due to heat strokes, changes in distribution of diseases borne by vectors (such as mosquitos and ticks), poorer air quality as warmer temperatures increase the formation of ozone, lower agricultural productivity, and water and food stress.

Ecosystem Impact

Rising sea levels, warmer temperatures, changes in distribution of diseases borne by vectors (such as mosquitos and ticks), forest fires, water and food related stress impact all life on Earth. A recent U.N. report revealed that a million species are in danger of extinction with climate change being a major driver.

Regulations

In 2015, 185 nations signed the Paris Agreement to lower emissions of GHGs. In 2017, President Trump withdrew the U.S. support of the Paris Accord. In spite of this, some states in the US have committed to lower emissions of GHGs.
